  • What are cryptocurrencies
    A cryptocurrency is a virtual currency created thanks to a computerized code. Similarly, a cryptocurrency is a string of data that indicates a unit. The main difference between the latter and conventional currencies is that cryptocurrencies are completely decentralized, that is, they are not controlled or supervised by any government, they are monitored by a P2P Internet protocol. These digital currencies are created through a process called “mining”, where the miners add the records of the operations that are carried out to the public ledger of a certain cryptocurrency. All transactions occur immediately on each of the platforms and are added to the public ledger, where they can be viewed by anyone on the network. Once the miner confirms the transaction, it will be effective and cannot be returned, canceled or falsified. Through all this they have positioned them as one of the best assets for doing business.
